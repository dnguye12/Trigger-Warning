About This Project

This project explores school shootings in the United States from 1990 to the present. It looks at when and where incidents happen, and how severe they are.

What we study

We focus on incident severity, measured by the number of fatalities and injuries. We also compare factors like time range and school level.

Why it matters

School shootings affect students, teachers and communities in many ways. Long-term data can help people understand risk patterns and talk about safety with more clarity.

This page is for learning and awareness. It does not include graphic content.

Design choice

  • We use a red splatter mark to represent an incident.
  • The colour is a clear symbol of blood and harm, so it reminds viewers that the data describes real violence, not just numbers.
  • We keep the page non-graphic, but we want the design to show the seriousness of each event.

Our research questions

Question 1

How has severity changed over time since 1990, and are there periods of increase or decrease?

Question 2

How do school level work together to influence severity?

Question 3

How do timing factors (year, month, time of day) relate to school type, location, and severity?

We use these questions to guide the design of our visualization and to keep the focus on clear, testable patterns.
